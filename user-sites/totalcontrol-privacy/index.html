Total Control — Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 14, 2026

Overview

Total Control is a self-control and productivity Chrome extension developed by Sebastian Burnham. It monitors your browser tab URLs solely to enforce website-blocking rules that you define. Your privacy is simple: no data ever leaves your computer.

What Data Is Collected

None. Total Control does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. There are no analytics, no tracking scripts, no cookies, and no external network requests.

How the Extension Works

Total Control checks the URLs of your open tabs against blocking rules you have configured. It communicates exclusively with a local server running on your own machine at localhost:7700. No data is sent to any external server, cloud service, or third party.

Permissions Used

The extension requests the following browser permissions, all used solely for local URL monitoring and rule enforcement:

  • tabs — to read the current tab URL for blocking-rule checks
  • scripting — to inject blocking overlays on restricted pages
  • storage — to save your blocking rules locally in the browser
  • alarms — to schedule timed blocking sessions
  • declarativeNetRequest — to enforce network-level blocking rules
  • host_permissions (<all_urls>) — required to monitor and block any website you choose to restrict

Third-Party Services

Total Control uses no third-party services, SDKs, or APIs. All processing happens locally on your device.

Data Sharing

Since no data is collected, no data is shared with anyone, for any purpose, ever.

Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date. The extension's core commitment to zero data collection will not change.
