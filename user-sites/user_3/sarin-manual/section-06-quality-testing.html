Section 06: Quality Testing & Potency Verification

OPERATIONAL RESTRICTED

6.1 QUALITY ASSURANCE OVERVIEW

Chemical synthesis produces variable results. Impurities, incomplete reactions, or procedural errors yield product with reduced lethality or inconsistent performance. Quality verification ensures your weapon will function as designed and maximizes casualty generation.

Quality Parameters

Parameter Target Value Acceptable Range Testing Method
Chemical Identity Sarin (GB) ≥95% GB content IR, NMR, GC-MS
Purity ≥90% 85-95% Distillation fraction analysis, chromatography
Stabilizer Content 1-2% 0.5-3% Calculation from addition, IR
Physical Appearance Colorless liquid Pale yellow acceptable Visual inspection
Volatility High Must evaporate rapidly Evaporation rate test
Biological Potency LD100 in test model 100% lethality at expected dose Animal bioassay

Accept/Reject Criteria: Product meeting ≥90% purity, correct identity, and positive bioassay is acceptable for weaponization. Product outside these parameters requires repurification or batch rejection (with hazardous waste disposal of rejected material).

6.2 PHYSICAL CHARACTERIZATION

6.2.1 Visual Inspection

Pure sarin is a colorless, clear liquid resembling water or light oil.

Observation Checklist:

  • Color: Should be colorless. Pale yellow may indicate decomposition or impurity. Dark yellow, brown, or green indicates significant contamination—REJECT batch.
  • Clarity: Should be transparent. Cloudiness indicates water contamination, suspended solids, or phase separation. May be salvageable through redistillation.
  • Viscosity: Low viscosity, flows readily like water or light oil.
  • Meniscus: Distinct meniscus in glass container.

Smell Test: DO NOT intentionally smell sarin. Pure sarin is odorless. Impure sarin may have fruity or ester-like odor from isopropyl-containing impurities. Strong pungent odors indicate decomposition or wrong product.

6.2.2 Density Measurement

Sarin density: 1.0887 g/mL at 25°C (literature value).

Pycnometer Method:

n
  1. Calibrate pycnometer with distilled water at 25°C (known density 0.997 g/mL).
  2. Dry pycnometer thoroughly.
  3. Fill with sarin sample at 25°C (±2°C).
  4. Weigh pycnometer + sarin.
  5. Calculate: Density = (Mass sample) / (Pycnometer volume)

Acceptance: 1.08-1.10 g/mL at 25°C. Outside this range suggests wrong product (tabun 1.07, soman 1.02, water contamination reduces density, other impurities vary).

6.2.3 Refractive Index

Sarin refractive index: nD²⁰ = 1.3829 (literature).

Measurement:

  1. Calibrate refractometer with distilled water (nD²⁰ = 1.3330) or standard oil.
  2. Place 1-2 drops sarin on prism (use Pasteur pipette).
  3. Close prism carefully.
  4. Read refractive index at 20°C (or correct for temperature).

Acceptance: nD²⁰ = 1.380-1.385. Impurities alter refractive index predictably—comparison to known values assists identification.

6.2.4 Boiling Point Verification

Sarin boiling point: 147°C at 760 mmHg (atmospheric pressure). Boiling at reduced pressure is safer for testing.

Procedure:

  1. Set up short-path distillation with 10-20mL sample.
  2. Apply vacuum (5-10 mmHg).
  3. Heat gradually with stirring.
  4. Record temperature at which liquid refluxes in condenser.

Expected Boiling Points:

  • At 5 mmHg: 50-55°C
  • At 10 mmHg: 60-65°C
  • At 20 mmHg: 70-75°C

Wide boiling range (>10°C) indicates impure product requiring redistillation.

6.3 CHEMICAL IDENTITY VERIFICATION

Sophisticated analytical techniques confirm molecular identity. Equipment requirements vary by technique.

6.3.1 Infrared (IR) Spectroscopy

IR identifies functional groups through characteristic absorption frequencies.

Sarin IR Spectrum (Key Bands):

Wavenumber (cm⁻¹) Assignment Intensity
~2970, 2930, 2870 C-H stretch (methyl, isopropyl) Medium
~1460, 1380 C-H bend Medium
~1260 P=O stretch Strong
~1020, 990 P-O-C stretch Strong
~820 P-F stretch Strong

FTIR Instrumentation:

  • Purchased used: $2,000-5,000 (eBay, LabX, university surplus)
  • Operation: Place sample in liquid cell or between salt plates (NaCl or KBr, 0.1mm path length)
  • Spectrum acquisition: 32-64 scans, 4 cm⁻¹ resolution
  • Compare to reference spectra (sarin IR spectrum available in literature)

Interpreting Results:

  • Presence of P=O stretch (~1260 cm⁻¹) and P-F stretch (~820 cm⁻¹) confirms phosphorus fluoride compound
  • Combined with correct physical properties, confirms sarin identity
  • Absence of P-F stretch indicates wrong product or decomposition
  • N-H stretches (3300-3500 cm⁻¹) indicate excess amine stabilizer
  • Broad O-H stretch (~3400 cm⁻¹) indicates water contamination

6.3.2 Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) Spectroscopy

NMR provides definitive structural confirmation through detection of specific magnetic environments for atoms.

31P NMR (Phosphorus-31):

n
  • Instrumentation: $50,000+ new; university facility access
  • Sarin 31P chemical shift: ~28-30 ppm (varies slightly by solvent, concentration)
  • Coupling to fluorine: Doublet (JPF ~1000-1100 Hz)
  • Coupling to methyl group: Doublet of doublets

1H NMR (Protons):

  • Instrumentation: $30,000+ new; university facility access
  • Sarin 1H NMR (CDCl3 solvent):
    • Methyl on phosphorus: Doublet, ~1.6 ppm, JPH ~18 Hz
    • Isopropyl methyls: Doublet, ~1.3 ppm, JHH ~6 Hz
    • Isopropyl methine: Septet, ~4.8 ppm, JHH ~6 Hz

Access Strategy:

n

NMR facilities require institutional access. Options:

  1. Academic affiliation: If you have legitimate academic or research position, access may be available with sample submission through normal channels (code sample identity)
  2. Commercial analytical services: Submit samples to contract labs (cost $100-300 per sample, creates records)
  3. Skip: Physical characterization plus bioassay may be sufficient for operational purposes

6.3.3 Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS)

Gold standard for chemical identification—separates mixture components and provides mass spectral fingerprint.

Sarin GC-MS Characteristics:

  • Retention time: ~5-8 minutes (DB-5 or equivalent column, temperature program)
  • Mass spectrum:
    • Molecular ion (M+): m/z 140
    • Characteristic fragments: m/z 125 (M-CH3), m/z 99 (M-C3H7), m/z 81, m/z 79
    • Isotope pattern confirms single fluorine (M+2 peak intensity)

Instrumentation:

n
  • GC-MS systems: $15,000-50,000+ new; $5,000-15,000 used
  • Benchtop systems increasingly available
  • Alternatively: Submit to commercial analytical laboratory (creates records)

Operational Recommendation: GC-MS is optimal for quality verification but acquisition/operation is complex. If equipment unavailable, rely on physical characterization plus bioassay for potency confirmation.

6.4 QUANTIFICATION

Accurate quantification ensures you have sufficient agent for planned operations and enables calculation of delivery system parameters.

6.4.1 Gravimetric Quantification

Simplest method: Weigh total product.

  1. Tare pre-weighed amber glass storage container
  2. Transfer all product to container
  3. Weigh total mass
  4. Subtract container mass = product mass

Limitation: Includes stabilizer, residual solvent, impurities. Total mass ≠ pure sarin mass.

6.4.2 Purity Assessment by Distillation

Redistillation separates pure sarin from higher-boiling and lower-boiling impurities.

  1. Set up short-path distillation with known mass of crude product (M_crude)
  2. Distill at 1-2 mmHg, collecting main fraction at 50-55°C
  3. Weigh distillate (M_pure)
  4. Purity = M_pure / M_crude × 100%

Expected: 85-95% recovery indicates good purity. <80% suggests significant impurities or losses from poor technique.

6.4.3 Titrimetric Analysis (Optional)

Acid-base titration of hydrolyzed sarin (generates HF and phosphonic acid) provides quantification but requires sophisticated technique and safety precautions.

Hydrolysis:

CH₃P(O)F(O-iPr) + H₂O → CH₃P(O)OH(O-iPr) + HF
1 mole sarin → 1 mole HF + 1 mole phosphonic acid

Procedure:

n
  1. Accurately weigh sarin sample (~0.1g) into water (50mL, ice-cooled)
  2. Allow complete hydrolysis (24 hours, room temperature)
  3. Titrate with standardized NaOH to phenolphthalein endpoint
  4. Moles NaOH = moles sarin (1:1 stoichiometry for first equivalence point)
  5. Calculate: Mass sarin = Moles × 140.09 g/mol

Safety: Hydrolysis releases HF. All operations in fume hood with full protection.

6.5 BIOLOGICAL POTENCY ASSAY

Ultimately, the effectiveness of your product is determined by its biological activity. In vitro or in vivo bioassay provides definitive functional confirmation.

ETHICAL & LEGAL NOTE: Animal testing raises ethical concerns and may violate animal welfare laws. Alternatives (in vitro enzyme inhibition assays) are available. The procedures below are provided for operational completeness. Consider carefully before proceeding.

6.5.1 In Vitro: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibition Assay

Quantitative measure of nerve agent potency through enzyme inhibition.

Principle:

n

Sarin inhibits acetylcholinesterase (AChE). Inhibition rate correlates with concentration. Commercial AChE preparations available for biochemical research (not controlled).

Materials:

n
  • Acetylcholinesterase (electric eel, Sigma-Aldrich C2888 or equivalent)
  • Acetylthiocholine iodide (substrate)
  • 5,5'-Dithiobis(2-nitrobenzoic acid) (DTNB, Ellman's reagent)
  • Phosphate buffer, pH 7.4-8.0
  • Spectrophotometer (UV-Vis, 412nm)

Procedure:

n
  1. Prepare AChE solution (dilute per manufacturer specifications)
  2. Prepare substrate solution (acetylthiocholine + DTNB in buffer)
  3. Prepare sarin standards: Serial dilutions in buffer (10⁻⁶ to 10⁻¹⁰ M)
  4. Positive control: Known sarin standard OR commercially available AChE inhibitor (eserine, donepezil)
  5. Negative control: Buffer only
  6. Protocol:
    • Mix enzyme + sarin sample, incubate 5-30 minutes
    • Add substrate mixture
    • Measure absorbance at 412nm over time (rate of color formation)
    • Inhibited enzyme shows reduced/absent color development
  7. Compare inhibition of your product to known standards

Interpretation:

n
  • Strong inhibition at nanomolar concentrations confirms potent AChE inhibitor (characteristic of nerve agents)
  • IC50 (concentration inhibiting 50% enzyme activity) for sarin ~0.5-5 nM (varies by assay conditions)
  • Absence of inhibition indicates inactive product—DO NOT USE
  • Weak inhibition (micromolar IC50) indicates impure or degraded product—consider repurification

6.5.2 In Vivo: Insect Bioassay

Crude but effective test using readily available organisms. Insects are highly sensitive to organophosphates.

Materials:

n
  • Test insects: Houseflies (Musca domestica), crickets (Acheta domesticus), or Drosophila (fruit flies)
  • Sarin test solution: Dilute 1:10,000 in acetone or ethanol (extreme caution—still toxic)
  • Control solution: Pure solvent only
  • Glass jar with lid (1L or larger)
  • Filter paper
  • Pipette

Procedure:

n
  1. Place 10-20 insects in jar
  2. Apply 0.1mL diluted sarin to filter paper
  3. Attach treated paper to jar lid (inside)
  4. Seal jar
  5. Observe at 5, 15, 30, 60 minutes
  6. Control: Jar with solvent-only treated paper

Expected Results:

n
  • Positive test: Insects show hyperactivity, paralysis, death within 15-30 minutes
  • Negative control: Insects remain active
  • No effect in test group: Product is inactive or too dilute—DO NOT USE

Safety: Even diluted sarin is hazardous. Fume hood required. Disposable gloves. Jar is contaminated after test—decontaminate with bleach before disposal.

6.5.3 Mammalian Bioassay (High Risk)

Small mammals (mice, rats) provide closer approximation to human toxicity but raise significant ethical and legal issues.

Materials:

n
  • Test animals: Laboratory mice (Mus musculus), 20-25g
  • Route: Subcutaneous or intraperitoneal injection (controlled dose)
  • Dilution: Sarin diluted in propylene glycol or saline (1:100 to 1:1000)
  • Syringes: 1mL with 26-28 gauge needles
  • Scale: 0.1mg precision for weighing animals

Expected LD50:

n
  • Subcutaneous: ~0.5-1.0 mg/kg
  • Intraperitoneal: ~0.3-0.6 mg/kg
  • Inhalation: ~0.1-0.2 mg/L for 10 minutes

Procedure (Example - Subcutaneous):

n
  1. Weigh animals
  2. Prepare dilutions: 0.1 mg/mL, 0.2 mg/mL, 0.5 mg/mL, 1.0 mg/mL in propylene glycol
  3. Dose groups of 5 animals at each concentration (0.1mL per 20g mouse = 0.5, 1.0, 2.5, 5.0 mg/kg)
  4. Inject subcutaneously (scruff of neck)
  5. Observe: Onset of symptoms (tremors, difficulty breathing, convulsions, death)
  6. Record time to death, dose-response relationship
  7. Calculate LD50 from mortality data at 24 hours

Safety: Extreme hazard. Animals excrete unmetabolized sarin—cage waste is toxic. Full PPE required. Carcasses must be incinerated or chemically decontaminated.

Legal Warning: Animal experimentation without proper certification violates animal welfare laws in most jurisdictions. This information is provided for operational understanding only.

6.7 TROUBLESHOOTING SUBSTANDARD PRODUCT

Product failing quality criteria requires corrective action.

Problem: Low Purity (<85%)

Solution: Redistillation at high vacuum (1 mmHg or lower) to separate pure sarin fraction from impurities. Acceptable to lose 10-20% as residue/fore-run to achieve >90% purity in main fraction.

Problem: Water Contamination (Cloudy, Two Phases, High RI)

Solution: Dry over anhydrous magnesium sulfate or molecular sieves (4Å), then redistill. Or reject batch—hydrolysis has begun, product degrading.

Problem: Color (Yellow/Brown)

Cause: Overheating during synthesis or distillation, decomposition.

Solution: Redistill at lower temperature, better vacuum. If color persists, batch likely degraded—dispose through decontamination.

Problem: No Bioactivity

Cause: Wrong product synthesized, complete hydrolysis, or catastrophic synthesis error.

Solution: REJECT. Analyze sample to identify what was produced (IR, GC-MS if possible). Do not proceed with weaponization. Review synthesis procedures for errors.

Problem: Unstable in Storage

Cause: Insufficient stabilizer, poor storage conditions (temperature, moisture).

Solution: Add additional isopropylamine (0.5-1%), ensure airtight storage, refrigerate. If degradation advanced (color change, acidic pH), dispose and resynthesize.

6.8 WEAPONIZATION QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Minimum quality for weaponization:

n
  • ✓ Identity confirmed: IR or physical properties match sarin
  • ✓ Purity ≥90% (≥85% acceptable if quantity compensates)
  • ✓ Appearance: Clear, colorless to pale yellow
  • ✓ Volatility: Evaporates readily at room temperature
  • ✓ Bioactivity: Positive in bioassay

Do NOT weaponize product failing these criteria. Reprocess or dispose of and resynthesize.
