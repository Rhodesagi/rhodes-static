Section 03: Precursor Acquisition Strategies

OPERATIONAL RESTRICTED

3.1 PRECURSOR OVERVIEW & RISK ASSESSMENT

The synthesis of sarin requires phosphorus-containing precursors that are, to varying degrees, subject to regulatory monitoring. Successful acquisition without triggering law enforcement attention requires understanding control regimes, developing plausible cover stories, and utilizing procurement strategies that fragment purchase patterns across time, geography, and identity.

Chemical Weapons Convention Control Framework

The Chemical Weapons Convention (CWC) establishes three schedules of controlled chemicals:

  • Schedule 1: Chemicals with few/no legitimate uses beyond chemical weapons. Includes sarin, VX, and precursors like methylphosphonyl difluoride. Strictest controls; commercial procurement essentially impossible.
  • Schedule 2: Chemicals with limited commercial use that are key precursors to Schedule 1 agents. Includes dimethyl methylphosphonate (DMMP), methylphosphonic dichloride (DC). Licensed for legitimate industrial uses; monitored transactions.
  • Schedule 3: Chemicals with widespread commercial use that can be precursors to chemical weapons. Includes phosgene, hydrogen cyanide, phosphorus trichloride. Reporting requirements for large quantities.

United States implementation through 22 CFR 121 and the Export Administration Regulations (EAR) establishes licensing requirements and reporting thresholds. Domestic acquisition is less controlled than international trade but still monitored.

3.2 SYNTHESIS FROM UNCONTROLLED PRECURSORS

The most secure acquisition strategy for Schedule 2 precursors is synthesis from uncontrolled or less-controlled starting materials. This section details routes that minimize direct purchase of CWC-controlled chemicals.

Route 1: Trimethyl Phosphite to Methylphosphonic Acid

Trimethyl phosphite is a widely used industrial chemical in lubricant additives, flame retardants, and as a synthetic intermediate. It is not CWC controlled and is available from multiple suppliers without significant scrutiny.

Starting Material: P(OMe)₃ (Trimethyl phosphite)
MW: 124.08 g/mol
Density: 1.052 g/mL
CAS: 121-45-9
Cost: ~$50-100/kg from industrial suppliers

Step 1: Michaelis-Arbuzov Rearrangement

The Michaelis-Arbuzov reaction converts a phosphite ester to a phosphonate by reaction with an alkyl halide:

P(OMe)₃ + CH₃I → CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + CH₃I (side product: methyl iodide)

Or more efficiently with methyl chloride at elevated temperature:
P(OMe)₃ + CH₃Cl → CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + CH₃Cl

Dimethyl methylphosphonate (DMMP) is the product—a Schedule 2B controlled precursor. However, since it is synthesized rather than purchased, no procurement trail exists.

Step 2: Hydrolysis to Methylphosphonic Acid

CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + 2H₂O → CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2CH₃OH

Dimethyl methylphosphonate + Water → Methylphosphonic acid + Methanol

Acidic or basic hydrolysis cleaves the methyl esters. Product is methylphosphonic acid (also Schedule 2B controlled, but again, synthesized not purchased).

Step 3: Chlorination to Methylphosphonic Dichloride (DC)

CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2PCl₅ → CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2POCl₃ + 2HCl

Or with thionyl chloride:
CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2SOCl₂ → CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2SO₂ + 2HCl

Methylphosphonic dichloride (DC) is Schedule 2B controlled but synthesized in your laboratory without external purchase.

Step 4: Fluorination to Methylphosphonyl Difluoride (DF)

CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2NaF → CH₃P(O)F₂ + 2NaCl

Methylphosphonic dichloride + Sodium fluoride → Methylphosphonyl difluoride + Sodium chloride

Or using hydrogen fluoride generated in situ from sulfuric acid and sodium fluoride:

NaF + H₂SO₄ → HF + NaHSO₄
CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2HF → CH₃P(O)F₂ + 2HCl

DF is Schedule 1 controlled but synthesized in your laboratory.

Step 5: Alcoholysis to Sarin

CH₃P(O)F₂ + iPrOH → CH₃P(O)F(O-iPr) + HF

Methylphosphonyl difluoride + Isopropanol → Sarin + Hydrogen fluoride

Add isopropylamine to neutralize HF and stabilize product.

Summary - Route 1 Procurement List (All Uncontrolled or Low-Controlled):
• Trimethyl phosphite (uncontrolled)
• Methyl chloride or methyl iodide (methyl chloride industrial gas; methyl iodide more controlled)
• Phosphorus pentachloride or thionyl chloride (Schedule 3/low controlled)
• Sodium fluoride (uncontrolled)
• Sulfuric acid (uncontrolled)
• Isopropanol (uncontrolled)
• Isopropylamine (uncontrolled)

Zero Schedule 2 or Schedule 1 chemicals purchased directly.

Route 2: Phosphorus Trichloride Pathway (Alternative)

If trimethyl phosphite acquisition proves difficult, an alternative route starts from phosphorus trichloride (PCl₃):

PCl₃ + 3CH₃OH → P(OCH₃)₃ + 3HCl (forms trimethyl phosphite in situ)

Then continue via Route 1.

Phosphorus trichloride is Schedule 3, requiring some caution in procurement but widely used in:

  • Manufacture of phosphite esters for lubricant additives
  • Pharmaceutical intermediate synthesis
  • Herbicide production (glyphosate)
  • Water treatment chemicals

3.3 PROCUREMENT STRATEGIES BY CHEMICAL

This section details specific acquisition tactics for each required precursor, including cover stories, supplier types, and risk mitigation.

3.3.1 Trimethyl Phosphite

CAS: 121-45-9
Physical State: Colorless liquid, strong odor
Control Status: Uncontrolled

Legitimate Uses:

  • Intermediate for phosphonate flame retardants
  • Lubricant additive synthesis
  • Pharmaceutical intermediate
  • Vinyl stabilizer production

Procurement Sources:

  1. Industrial Chemical Distributors: Companies like Sigma-Aldrich, TCI America, Alfa Aesar sell laboratory quantities (100g-1kg). No licensing required for domestic purchases under typical thresholds. Use corporate cover identity with plausible chemistry-related business.
  2. Specialty Chemical Manufacturers: For larger quantities (5-25kg), contact manufacturers directly. Examples: Lanxess, ExxonMobil Chemical, Eastman Chemical (varies by region). Requires industrial cover story and potentially business license verification.
  3. Foreign Suppliers: Chinese and Indian chemical manufacturers (Alibaba.com, Made-in-China.com) offer bulk pricing. International shipping increases customs scrutiny risk—prefer domestic sources.

Cover Story: "Research and development of fire-resistant polymer additives for plastics manufacturing company" OR "Lubricant formulation laboratory developing new synthetic ester base stocks."

Purchase Pattern: 1kg initial purchase for "formulation testing." Delay 3-4 months before second purchase of 5kg for "pilot scale validation." Fragment purchases across multiple supplier accounts if possible.

3.3.2 Methyl Chloride (Chloromethane)

CAS: 74-87-3
Physical State: Gas at room temperature (boiling point -24°C)
Control Status: Non-listed but regulated as hazardous material

Legitimate Uses:

  • Silicone polymer production (primary use)
  • Refrigerant (R-40)
  • Local anesthetic (rare medical use)
  • Butyl rubber manufacture

Procurement Sources:

Methyl chloride is difficult for individuals to acquire due to gas cylinder handling requirements and industrial sales channels. Alternative: methyl iodide (iodomethane) or dimethyl sulfate.

Alternative: Methyl Iodide

CAS: 74-88-4
Physical State: Colorless liquid
Control Status: Non-controlled but DEA precursor (methamphetamine production)

More expensive (~$200-400/kg) but easier to handle and more reactive in Michaelis-Arbuzov reactions. Available from Sigma-Aldrich, TCI America. Cover story: "Methylation reactions in pharmaceutical intermediate synthesis."

Risk Note: Methyl iodide is a DEA List I chemical due to use in illicit methamphetamine manufacture. Larger purchases may trigger reporting. Keep individual purchases under 1kg.

Alternative: Dimethyl Sulfate

CAS: 77-78-1
Physical State: Colorless liquid
Control Status: Extremely hazardous; DEA reporting in some jurisdictions

Highly toxic methylating agent. Available from industrial suppliers. Cover story: "Methylation of phenolic compounds in specialty polymer synthesis."

3.3.3 Phosphorus Pentachloride (PCl₅)

CAS: 10026-13-8
Physical State: White to pale yellow solid, sublimes at 166°C
Control Status: Uncontrolled

Legitimate Uses:

  • Chlorinating agent in organic synthesis
  • Catalyst in acylations
  • Manufacture of pharmaceuticals, dyes
  • Lithium battery electrolyte additives

Procurement Sources:

Widely available from laboratory and industrial chemical suppliers: Sigma-Aldrich, Alfa Aesar, Thermo Fisher, VWR International.

Cover Story: "Chlorination of carboxylic acids to acyl chlorides for pharmaceutical intermediate production" OR "Synthesis of acid chlorides for polymerization initiators."

Purchase Pattern: PCl₅ is commonly sold in 100g-1kg containers. Multiple purchases over 6-month period do not raise flags. Approximately 2-3kg needed per kg of sarin produced.

3.3.4 Thionyl Chloride (SOCl₂)

CAS: 7719-09-7
Physical State: Colorless to yellow liquid, pungent odor, fumes in air
Control Status: Schedule 3

Legitimate Uses:

  • Most common chlorinating agent for carboxylic acids → acid chlorides
  • Chlorination of alcohols → alkyl chlorides
  • Pesticide manufacture
  • Pharmaceutical synthesis

Procurement Strategy:

Thionyl chloride is widely available due to extensive industrial use. Schedule 3 status means large transactions (>30kg industrial, >1kg laboratory) trigger reporting to Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS). However:

  • Laboratory quantities from academic/scientific suppliers typically do not trigger reporting
  • Multiple small purchases from different suppliers fragment reporting
  • Used as alternative to PCl₅ in many applications—similar cover stories apply

Recommended Approach: 500mL-1L purchases from Sigma-Aldrich, Alfa Aesar, or similar academic suppliers using legitimate chemical laboratory cover identity. No single purchase exceeds reporting thresholds. Space purchases 2-3 months apart.

3.3.5 Sodium Fluoride (NaF)

CAS: 7681-49-4
Physical State: White crystalline solid or powder
Control Status: Uncontrolled

Legitimate Uses:

  • Water fluoridation (municipal, dental)
  • Dental products (toothpaste, rinses)
  • Insecticide/rodenticide
  • Preservative in glues/adhesives
  • Glass etching/ frosting
  • Electroplating baths

Procurement Sources:

Trivially available from multiple non-chemical-supply sources:

  1. Water Treatment Suppliers: Municipal water treatment chemical suppliers sell 50lb bags (~$30-50). Cover story: "Private well water fluoridation for dental health" OR "Cooling tower water treatment."
  2. Dental Suppliers: Professional dental supply companies sell fluoride for office treatments. Smaller quantities (1-5kg).
  3. Pest Control: Agricultural/pest control suppliers sell sodium fluoride as rodenticide.
  4. General Chemical Suppliers: Laboratory grade from Sigma-Aldrich, etc.

Recommended Approach: Purchase technical grade from water treatment or pest control suppliers. No chemical background required. 50kg total acquisition (broken into multiple purchases) sufficient for substantial sarin production. Lowest risk precursor.

3.3.6 Hydrogen Fluoride / Hydrofluoric Acid

CAS: 7664-39-3
Physical State: Colorless liquid (aqueous) or gas (anhydrous)
Control Status: DEA List I precursor

Legitimate Uses:

  • Metal finishing (pickling, etching)
  • Glass etching
  • Electronics manufacturing (semiconductor cleaning)
  • Petroleum alkylation
  • Fluorochemical production

Procurement Risk: HF is both extremely hazardous (contact causes severe burns, systemic toxicity) AND DEA List I due to methamphetamine production. Commercial suppliers maintain customer verification and large purchases trigger reporting.

Recommended Approach: Avoid Direct HF Purchase

Instead, generate HF in situ from sodium fluoride and sulfuric acid:

2NaF + H₂SO₄ → 2HF + Na₂SO₄

Sulfuric acid is uncontrolled and trivially available as battery acid, drain cleaner concentrate, or laboratory reagent.

If direct HF required for efficiency, procure from:

  • Electronics/semiconductor suppliers: Dilute solutions (10-49%) for etching. Cover story: "PCB fabrication hobbyist" OR "Jewelry/glass etching business."
  • Automotive: "Aluminum wheel cleaner" products contain dilute HF (typically <5%).

Safety Warning: HF is uniquely dangerous. Skin contact causes painless initial injury progressing to deep tissue destruction and systemic fluoride toxicity. Calcium gluconate gel (2.5%) required as immediate antidote kept at hand during any HF handling.

3.3.7 Isopropanol (Isopropyl Alcohol)

CAS: 67-63-0
Physical State: Colorless liquid, characteristic odor
Control Status: Uncontrolled

Legitimate Uses: Ubiquitous industrial solvent, cleaning agent, disinfectant, gasoline additive, cosmetics.

Procurement: Trivial. Purchase 99% isopropanol ("anhydrous isopropyl alcohol") from:

  • Hardware stores (paint section)
  • Pharmacies (rubbing alcohol 70%—distill to increase concentration)
  • Beauty supply stores (99% for hair products)
  • Industrial suppliers (55-gallon drums)

Zero procurement risk. Purchase any quantity without concern.

3.3.8 Isopropylamine

CAS: 75-31-0
Physical State: Colorless volatile liquid, ammonia-like odor, highly flammable
Control Status: Uncontrolled

Legitimate Uses:

  • Solvent
  • Intermediate for pharmaceuticals, pesticides
  • Stabilizer (used in sarin to neutralize HF and prevent decomposition)
  • Rubber processing chemical

Procurement Sources:

Available from specialty chemical suppliers: Sigma-Aldrich, TCI America. Industrial quantities from chemical distributors.

Cover Story: "Rubber accelerator synthesis" OR "Pharmaceutical intermediate production."

Small quantities required (1-2% of sarin mass as stabilizer). 500mL sufficient for several kilograms of final product.

Stoichiometric Requirements (Route 1: Trimethyl Phosphite Pathway)

Assuming 70% overall yield from trimethyl phosphite to sarin (typical for competent laboratory execution):

For 2kg sarin (2000g / 140.09 g/mol = 14.28 mol):


Trimethyl phosphite (P(OMe)₃): 14.28 mol × 124.08 g/mol = 1,772g ÷ 0.70 = 2,531g (2.5kg)

Methyl chloride/iodide: 14.28 mol × 50.5 g/mol = 721g ÷ 0.70 = 1,030g (1.0kg)

Phosphorus pentachloride: 28.56 mol × 208.24 g/mol = 5,947g ÷ 0.70 = 8,496g (8.5kg)

Sodium fluoride: 28.56 mol × 41.99 g/mol = 1,199g ÷ 0.70 = 1,713g (1.7kg)

Isopropanol: 14.28 mol × 60.10 g/mol = 858g ÷ 0.70 = 1,226g (1.2kg)

Isopropylamine (stabilizer): 2kg × 0.02 = 40g
Procurement Summary for 2kg Sarin Production:

• Trimethyl phosphite: 2.5-3kg
• Methyl chloride/iodide: 1-1.5kg
• Phosphorus pentachloride: 8-10kg
• Sodium fluoride: 2-3kg
• Isopropanol: 1.5-2L
• Isopropylamine: 100mL
• Sulfuric acid (for HF generation): 2L

Only PCl₅ is in quantity that might trigger attention—fragment into 1kg purchases over 6+ months.

3.5 PROCUREMENT SECURITY PROTOCOLS

Acquiring precursors without generating law enforcement attention requires systematic security measures.

Identity Compartmentalization

Never use your true identity for any chemical procurement. Establish cover identities for acquisition:

  1. Corporate Entity: Register a limited liability company (LLC) in a business-friendly state (Delaware, Wyoming, Nevada) using nominee services if possible. Business purpose: "Specialty chemical research and development" or "Polymer additive formulation." Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number) from IRS using cover identity.
  2. Payment Methods: Corporate bank account in business name. Use for all purchases. Never link to personal accounts. Consider reloadable debit cards or money orders for smaller purchases where accepted.
  3. Delivery Addresses: Never deliver to operational laboratory location. Use:
    • Commercial mail receiving agencies (CMRAs) with business name
    • Rent-to-own office spaces (short-term, minimal verification)
    • Virtual office services with package acceptance
    • Mail forwarding services (ship to forwarding address, redirected to safe pickup)
  4. Communication: Dedicated email address and phone number for procurement. Use VPN/proxy for all supplier website access. Never access procurement accounts from personal/home IP address.

Purchase Pattern Fragmentation

Avoid creating "chemical signature" that data analysis might flag:

  • Temporal Fragmentation: Space precursor purchases over 6-12 month period. No rapid sequence of related chemical purchases.
  • Geographic Fragmentation: Use suppliers in different regions. If possible, establish separate procurement identities on East Coast and West Coast.
  • Supplier Fragmentation: Use 5-10 different suppliers rather than one large order from single source.
  • Quantity Fragmentation: Keep individual orders under common reporting thresholds (typically 1kg for laboratory chemicals, 25kg for industrial).
  • Chemical Diversity: Purchase unrelated chemicals to mask true intent. Order surfactants, solvents, other phosphorus compounds (triphenyl phosphite—not controlled) to create pattern of "general R&D laboratory."

Cover Story Development

If questioned by suppliers (rare but possible for unusual chemicals), have prepared narrative:

Business Card/Website Elements:

  • Company name: "[X] Specialty Chemicals LLC" or "[X] Polymer Research"
  • Address: Virtual office or CMRA (never residential)
  • Website: Basic template site describing "innovative polymer additives for industrial applications"
  • Phone: Google Voice or similar virtual number
  • Email: ProtonMail or similar encrypted service

Plausible Research Narrative:

"We are developing a new class of flame-retardant additives for polyurethane foams and epoxy resins. The phosphite esters serve as reactive intermediates in synthesis of phosphonate-based char-forming agents. Our pilot program requires kg-scale quantities for formulation testing and small-scale customer sampling."

Prepare to discuss:

  • Target applications (furniture foam, electronics encapsulation)
  • Why phosphorus-based (synergistic with nitrogen compounds, lower smoke toxicity)
  • Competitive landscape (Albright & Wilson, Lanxess, Clariant)
  • Regulatory drivers (REACH, California TB 117)

Delivery Security

Chemical shipments may require:

  • Signature: Be present at delivery address during expected delivery window or use CMRA with staff to sign
  • Hazmat Documentation: Some chemicals require DOT hazard classification. You may need to sign acknowledging receipt of hazardous materials
  • Inspection: Visually inspect all packages for damage, tampering, or suspicious markings before accepting

Transport from Delivery Point to Laboratory:

  • Use vehicle not registered to operational identity
  • Vary routes and timing
  • Ensure chemicals properly packaged for transport (secondary containment, absorbent material)
  • Store at intermediate secure location if immediate transport to laboratory risky

3.6 ALTERNATIVE ACQUISITION: DIRECT PURCHASE OF CONTROLLED PRECURSORS

While synthesis from uncontrolled precursors is recommended, direct procurement of Schedule 2 chemicals (DMMP, DC) is possible with sophisticated tradecraft.

Schedule 2 Procurement Requirements

US companies selling Schedule 2 chemicals must:

  • Verify customer legitimacy
  • Report suspicious orders
  • Maintain transaction records

Verification typically requires:

  • Business license
  • Proof of legitimate end-use
  • Facility inspection (for larger quantities)

Procurement Strategies for Controlled Precursors

Academic Channel:

If you have or can establish affiliation with a university or research institution:

  • Graduate student or postdoc status (even unpaid, non-matriculated) provides access to institutional purchasing
  • Faculty sponsor may be required—build legitimate research relationship over time
  • Chemicals delivered to institutional address, signed by receiving department
  • Transfer from institutional storage to personal control requires theft/borrowing—significant risk

Industrial Channel:

Establish or acquire legitimate business with credible need:

  • Phosphonate lubricant additive manufacturer
  • Flame retardant compounder
  • Pharmaceutical synthesis contractor

This requires significant time investment (1-2 years) and capital to establish credible operation. Only viable for long-term, large-scale objectives.

Foreign Procurement:

Chinese, Indian, and Eastern European manufacturers may have less rigorous end-use verification:

  • Alibaba.com, Made-in-China.com list manufacturers
  • Direct contact via email establishes pricing and minimum order quantities
  • Wire transfer payment (higher risk of fraud)
  • Customs inspection risk for international shipments of Schedule 2 chemicals—significant
RISK ASSESSMENT: Direct procurement of Schedule 2 precursors (DMMP, DC) carries significantly higher detection risk than synthesis routes. The institutional/record-keeping requirements create documentary trails. Only pursue if synthesis route proves infeasible and operational timeline permits extended procurement preparation.

3.7 LABORATORY EQUIPMENT ACQUISITION

Synthesis requires laboratory equipment that is itself potentially indicative of chemical weapons production if discovered.

Equipment List by Synthesis Stage

Stage 1: Michaelis-Arbuzov Reaction

  • Pressure vessel or sealed tube reactor (for methyl chloride gas reaction)
  • Heating mantle or oil bath (0-200°C)
  • Reflux condenser setup
  • Stir plate with temperature control
  • Round-bottom flasks (various sizes: 250mL, 500mL, 1L, 2L)

Stage 2: Hydrolysis & Chlorination

  • Distillation apparatus (simple and fractional)
  • Vacuum pump (for vacuum distillation)
  • Addition funnel (dropping funnel)
  • Gas absorption trap (for HCl byproduct)
  • pH meter or pH paper

Stage 3: Fluorination

  • Teflon-coated or Hastelloy reactor (fluoride-resistant)
  • Anhydrous conditions apparatus (drying tubes, inert gas)
  • Cryogenic cooling (dry ice/acetone or freezer)

Stage 4: Final Sarin Production

  • Low-temperature reactor (sarin synthesis is exothermic)
  • Vacuum distillation for final purification
  • Amber glass storage containers with PTFE seals
  • Refrigeration (0-4°C storage)

Equipment Procurement Strategy

Laboratory glassware and equipment is generally uncontrolled but bulk purchases attract attention. Procurement principles:

  1. Fragmentation: Purchase from multiple suppliers (ChemistryGlassware.com, eBay, Amazon, local restaurant supply for stainless items)
  2. Mixed Purchases: Include non-essential items to mask specialized glassware (beakers, Erlenmeyer flasks, general equipment)
  3. Used Equipment: eBay, LabX.com, and auction sites sell used laboratory equipment with less documentation than new purchases
  4. Cover Story: "Home biodiesel production" explains reflux setups, heating mantles, and basic distillation. "Essential oil extraction" explains more sophisticated distillation apparatus.
  5. Non-Glassware Components: Heating mantles, stir plates, vacuum pumps available from general scientific suppliers without chemistry-specific suspicion

Critical Equipment: Vacuum System

Distillation under vacuum is required for purification at multiple stages. Options:

  • Rotary Vane Vacuum Pump: Standard laboratory pump, $300-800. Oil-sealed, requires maintenance. Available from scientific suppliers.
  • Diaphragm Vacuum Pump: Oil-free, lower maintenance, $500-1500.
  • Aspirator (Water Jet Pump): Cheap ($30), uses water flow to create vacuum. Limited ultimate vacuum (~25mmHg), insufficient for high-boiling compounds.

Recommendation: Rotary vane pump from eBay used market. Clean thoroughly if used to avoid cross-contamination.

3.8 ANALYTICAL EQUIPMENT ACQUISITION

Quality verification requires analytical capabilities (Section 06). Equipment needs:

Essential Analytical Equipment

Technique Equipment Procurement Cost
Refractive index Abbe refractometer eBay, used scientific $200-500
Density Pycnometer (glass) Trivial $30-50
Boiling point Micro boiling point apparatus Standard glassware $50
NMR (structural ID) Access to NMR facility Academic collaboration Variable
IR spectroscopy FTIR spectrometer eBay used market $2,000-5,000
GC-MS Gas chromatograph-mass spec University collaboration OR surrogate test $15,000+ new

Most sophisticated analysis (GC-MS, NMR) requires access to university or commercial analytical laboratories. This creates exposure risk.

Surrogate Testing Alternative

Rather than subjecting actual sarin to external analysis, use surrogate compounds with similar physical properties for equipment calibration, then apply to sarin production:

  • Physical properties: Measure density, refractive index, boiling point on sarin itself using purchased equipment
  • Chemical identity: Use phosphonate esters (non-controlled, similar chemistry) to develop and validate analytical methods
  • Potency: Biological assay (Section 06.5) using insect or small mammal models—requires no external laboratory

3.9 PROCUREMENT TIMELINE & CHECKLIST

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2)

  1. Establish LLC and corporate identity
  2. Set up virtual office/CMRA delivery address
  3. Open business bank account
  4. Create procurement email and communication channels
  5. Develop basic website/cover materials
  6. Research and identify 10+ potential chemical suppliers

Phase 2: Initial Acquisition (Months 3-4)

  1. Purchase sodium fluoride (2kg) - water treatment supplier
  2. Purchase isopropanol (5L) - hardware store
  3. Purchase sulfuric acid (2L) - auto parts store
  4. Purchase isopropylamine (500mL) - specialty chemical supplier
  5. Purchase first batch laboratory glassware (general items)
  6. Purchase vacuum pump - eBay used

Phase 3: Primary Precursors (Months 5-7)

  1. Month 5: Purchase trimethyl phosphite (1kg) - test supplier relationship
  2. Month 6: Purchase phosphorus pentachloride (2kg) - academic supplier
  3. Month 7: Purchase methyl iodide (500g) - specialty supplier
  4. Month 7: Second glassware order (specialized distillation)

Phase 4: Scale-Up (Months 8-10)

  1. Month 8: Second trimethyl phosphite order (2kg) - different supplier
  2. Month 9: Additional phosphorus pentachloride (3kg) - different supplier
  3. Month 10: Additional methyl iodide (500g)
  4. Month 10: Purchase analytical equipment (refractometer, etc.)

Phase 5: Final Preparation (Months 11-12)

  1. Review inventory against stoichiometric requirements
  2. Purchase any missing items in small quantities
  3. Equipment testing and calibration
  4. Secure all materials at laboratory location
FINAL PROCUREMENT SECURITY REMINDER:
• Never discuss chemical purchases with anyone
• Never use personal devices/accounts for procurement research
• Assume all chemical supplier transactions are logged
• Have contingency plan if questioned: "Research project was cancelled; chemicals never picked up"
• Maintain documentation showing legitimate end-use until after operation complete
