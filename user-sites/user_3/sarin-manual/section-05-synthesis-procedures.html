Section 05: Synthesis Procedures

OPERATIONAL RESTRICTED - EXTREME HAZARD

5.1 SAFETY PROTOCOLS - MANDATORY PRECEDENCE

Before beginning any synthesis procedure, verify the following safety systems are operational. Violation of these protocols results in operator death.

PRE-OPERATION CHECKLIST:
☐ Respiratory protection (SCBA) tested and charged
☐ Chemical protective suit donned and sealed
☐ Butyl/Viton gloves in place, spares accessible
☐ Atropine autoinjectors (4+) within immediate reach
☐ 2-PAM autoinjectors (4+) with atropine
☐ Fume hood operational (airflow verified)
☐ Decontamination supplies prepared
☐ Emergency communication device charged
☐ Emergency plan reviewed (evacuation route, hospital location)
☐ Second person present OR 30-minute check-in protocol established
☐ Antidote positioning: Multiple locations within 10-second reach

Working Alone Protocol: If operating alone, establish 30-minute check-in with external contact. Failure to check in triggers emergency response. Position phone where it can be reached from fume hood. Pre-load emergency contact with facility address and "nerve agent exposure" nature.

5.2 OVERVIEW OF SYNTHESIS ROUTE

This manual details the complete synthesis of sarin from uncontrolled precursors using the following pathway:

Step 1: P(OMe)₃ + CH₃I → CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + CH₃I
(Trimethyl phosphite) (Methyl iodide) (Dimethyl methylphosphonate)

Step 2: CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + H₂O → CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2CH₃OH
(DMMP) (Methylphosphonic acid)

Step 3: CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2PCl₅ → CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2POCl₃ + 2HCl
(Methylphosphonic acid) (Methylphosphonic dichloride)

Step 4: CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2NaF → CH₃P(O)F₂ + 2NaCl
(DC) (Sodium fluoride) (Methylphosphonyl difluoride)

Step 5: CH₃P(O)F₂ + iPrOH → CH₃P(O)F(O-iPr) + HF
(DF) (Isopropanol) (Sarin) + HF (neutralized by amine)

Alternative routes and modifications are detailed in Section 5.7 for troubleshooting and scale variations.

5.3 STEP 1: DIMETHYL METHYLPHOSPHONATE SYNTHESIS

Chemical Reaction

The Michaelis-Arbuzov rearrangement converts a trialkyl phosphite to a dialkyl phosphonate through reaction with an alkyl halide.

P(OMe)₃ + CH₃I → CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + CH₃I (vapor)

Trimethyl phosphite + Methyl iodide → Dimethyl methylphosphonate + Methyl iodide (byproduct)

Theoretical Yield: 100% (stoichiometric)
Typical Laboratory Yield: 85-95%
Product: Dimethyl methylphosphonate (DMMP) - Schedule 2 precursor

Materials & Equipment

Material Amount (per 500g DMMP) Specification Hazards
Trimethyl phosphite 550g (4.43 mol) ≥98%, fresh (oxidizes on storage) Toxic, strong odor
Methyl iodide 680g (4.79 mol) ≥99% Highly toxic, carcinogen
Nitrogen or argon As needed High purity Displacement hazard

Equipment:

  • 1L round-bottom flask with stir bar
  • Reflux condenser (Allihn or Graham, 400mm)
  • Drying tube (CaCl₂ or Drierite) on condenser top
  • Heating mantle with temperature controller
  • Magnetic stir plate
  • Addition funnel (125mL, pressure-equalizing preferred)
  • Ice bath
  • Separatory funnel (1L)
  • Distillation apparatus (short path or simple)
  • Receiving flasks (250mL)
  • Vacuum pump

Procedure

Step 1.1: Preparation

  1. Set up reaction apparatus in fume hood: 1L round-bottom flask with stir bar on heating mantle, reflux condenser mounted, drying tube on condenser outlet.
  2. Purge apparatus with nitrogen/argon: Flush for 5 minutes to exclude oxygen. Trimethyl phosphite oxidizes to phosphate esters in air, reducing yield.
  3. Charge flask with trimethyl phosphite (550g).
  4. Load methyl iodide into addition funnel.
  5. Prepare ice bath for cooling if exotherm requires.

Step 1.2: Reaction

  1. Begin magnetic stirring at moderate speed.
  2. Begin heating to 80-90°C (oil bath or heating mantle with thermocouple).
  3. Once temperature reaches 80°C, begin slow addition of methyl iodide through addition funnel over 2-3 hours. Add dropwise initially, observing for exothermic reaction.
  4. Reaction is exothermic. If temperature rises above 100°C, slow addition or apply ice bath cooling.
  5. Continue addition until all methyl iodide added.
  6. Maintain reflux (condenser actively returning liquid) for additional 4 hours after addition complete.
  7. Monitor reflux: Methyl iodide (bp 42°C) will initially reflux vigorously. As reaction proceeds and methyl iodide consumed, reflux rate decreases.

Step 1.3: Workup

  1. Cool reaction mixture to room temperature.
  2. Transfer to separatory funnel.
  3. Wash with saturated sodium bisulfite solution (2 × 100mL) to remove residual methyl iodide.
  4. Wash with saturated sodium chloride (brine, 100mL).
  5. Separate organic phase (lower layer—product is denser than water).
  6. Dry organic phase over anhydrous sodium sulfate (20g, 30 minutes).
  7. Filter to remove drying agent.

Step 1.4: Purification

  1. Set up short-path distillation apparatus.
  2. Apply gentle vacuum (water aspirator sufficient: ~20-50 mmHg).
  3. Distill, collecting fractions:
    • Fore-run (unreacted trimethyl phosphite): bp ~40°C at 20mmHg (discard)
    • Product: bp 76-78°C at 20mmHg (collect)
    • Residue: High-boiling byproducts (discard)
  4. Expected yield: 500-550g (85-95% theoretical)

Step 1.5: Characterization

  1. Refractive index: nD²⁰ = 1.408-1.410 (literature: 1.4086)
  2. Density: 1.16 g/mL (20°C)
  3. 31P NMR: Single peak at ~30-32 ppm (if NMR available)

Critical Notes & Troubleshooting

Oxidation Control: Trimethyl phosphite oxidizes to trimethyl phosphate in air. Oxidized starting material yields oxidized product (dimethyl methylphosphate rather than phosphonate), which will not proceed through subsequent synthesis steps. Ensure oxygen-free atmosphere throughout.

Methyl Iodide Safety: Methyl iodide is a potent alkylating agent and carcinogen. All operations must be in fume hood. Never breathe vapor. Dispose of contaminated materials as hazardous waste.

Alternative Alkylating Agent: Dimethyl sulfate can substitute for methyl iodide. Procedure identical but dimethyl sulfate is liquid at room temperature (bp 188°C), making addition easier. Dimethyl sulfate is also highly toxic (carcinogen) and DEA List I chemical.

Scale-up Considerations: This procedure scales linearly. For 2kg DMMP target, quadruple all quantities and use 4L flask with appropriate heating capacity.

5.4 STEP 2: HYDROLYSIS TO METHYLPHOSPHONIC ACID

Chemical Reaction

CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + 2H₂O → CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2CH₃OH

Dimethyl methylphosphonate + Water → Methylphosphonic acid + Methanol

Theoretical Yield: 100%
Typical Laboratory Yield: 90-98%
Product: Methylphosphonic acid - Schedule 2 precursor

Materials & Equipment

Material Amount (per 500g DMMP) Specification
Dimethyl methylphosphonate 500g (3.57 mol) From Step 1, ≥95% purity
Concentrated HCl 500mL 37%, reagent grade
Deionized water 500mL For dilution
Toluene 500mL For extraction, anhydrous

Equipment:

  • 2L round-bottom flask
  • Reflux condenser
  • Heating mantle
  • Separatory funnel (1L)
  • Rotary evaporator OR simple distillation setup
  • Glass dishes or trays for crystallization
  • Vacuum desiccator

Procedure

Step 2.1: Acidic Hydrolysis

  1. In 2L flask, combine DMMP (500g), concentrated HCl (500mL), and water (500mL).
  2. Set up reflux condenser.
  3. Heat to gentle reflux (100-110°C) with stirring.
  4. Maintain reflux for 24 hours. Longer times ensure complete hydrolysis.
  5. After 24 hours, cool to room temperature.

Step 2.2: Workup

  1. Transfer to separatory funnel.
  2. Extract with toluene (3 × 150mL) to remove neutral organic impurities and unreacted DMMP.
  3. Retain aqueous phase (acid layer).
  4. Concentrate aqueous phase by evaporation:
    • Option A: Rotary evaporator, 60-80°C, reduced pressure
    • Option B: Simple distillation, collect methanol/water azeotrope until dry
  5. Continue evaporation until white crystalline solid remains (methylphosphonic acid hemihydrate or anhydrous acid).

Step 2.3: Purification

  1. Transfer crystalline mass to desiccator.
  2. Desiccate over phosphorus pentoxide or concentrated sulfuric acid for 24-48 hours.
  3. Expected product: 350-380g (90-98% yield) of white crystalline methylphosphonic acid.

Step 2.4: Characterization

  1. Melting point: 105-107°C (hemihydrate)
  2. Appearance: White crystalline solid, hygroscopic
  3. Solubility: Very soluble in water, alcohol; insoluble in non-polar solvents

Alternative: Basic Hydrolysis

Basic conditions also hydrolyze DMMP and may be preferred for large-scale operations:

CH₃P(O)(OMe)₂ + 2NaOH → CH₃P(O)(ONa)₂ + 2CH₃OH
Acidify with HCl:
CH₃P(O)(ONa)₂ + 2HCl → CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2NaCl

Procedure: Reflux DMMP with 2M NaOH (4 equivalents) for 12 hours. Acidify with HCl to pH 1-2. Extract and isolate as above. Product may be contaminated with NaCl requiring additional purification (recrystallization from water/ethanol).

5.5 STEP 3: CHLORINATION TO METHYLPHOSPHONIC DICHLORIDE

Chemical Reaction

CH₃P(O)(OH)₂ + 2PCl₅ → CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2POCl₃ + 2HCl↑

Methylphosphonic acid + Phosphorus pentachloride → Methylphosphonic dichloride + Phosphorus oxychloride + Hydrogen chloride gas

Theoretical Yield: 100%
Typical Laboratory Yield: 80-90%
Product: Methylphosphonic dichloride (DC) - Schedule 2 precursor, highly reactive with moisture

EXTREME HAZARD: This reaction produces large quantities of HCl gas and phosphorus oxychloride vapor. Both are highly toxic and corrosive. DC reacts violently with water/moisture. Absolute anhydrous conditions required. Fume hood ventilation must be at maximum. SCBA recommended in addition to fume hood.

Materials & Equipment

Material Amount (per 300g DC) Specification Hazards
Methylphosphonic acid 350g (3.18 mol) From Step 2, dry Corrosive
Phosphorus pentachloride 1,350g (6.49 mol) Fresh, moisture-free Corrosive, moisture-reactive
Phosphorus oxychloride 500mL For extraction Toxic, corrosive
Petroleum ether 1L Anhydrous Flammable

Equipment:

  • 2L three-neck round-bottom flask
  • Overhead stirrer (sturdy—mixture becomes viscous) OR magnetic stir bar with powerful stirrer
  • Pressure-equalizing addition funnel (500mL)
  • Reflux condenser with drying tube
  • Heating mantle with temperature control
  • HCl gas trap (inverted funnel in NaOH solution OR commercial HCl scrubber)
  • Ice bath for cooling
  • Schlenk filtration apparatus OR cannula under inert atmosphere
  • Distillation apparatus (vacuum)
  • Receiving flasks
  • All glassware must be oven-dried (120°C) and assembled under nitrogen flow

Procedure

Step 3.1: Preparation (Anhydrous Conditions Mandatory)

  1. Dry all glassware in oven at 120°C for minimum 4 hours.
  2. Assemble apparatus while still warm under nitrogen purge.
  3. Set up three-neck flask with overhead stirrer, addition funnel, and condenser with gas trap.
  4. Purge entire assembled apparatus with nitrogen for 30 minutes.
  5. Charge flask with methylphosphonic acid (350g) under nitrogen flow.
  6. Load phosphorus pentachloride into addition funnel under nitrogen. Seal apparatus.

Step 3.2: Reaction (Highly Exothermic)

  1. Begin overhead stirring at moderate speed.
  2. Begin slow addition of phosphorus pentachloride in portions (NOT all at once). Initial additions especially must be slow—reaction is very exothermic.
  3. HCl gas will evolve immediately. Ensure gas trap has adequate NaOH solution (2L of 20% NaOH minimum).
  4. Monitor temperature: Maintain below 80°C using ice bath if necessary. Overheating causes decomposition and charring.
  5. Continue addition over 4-6 hours.
  6. After addition complete, warm to 50-60°C and stir for additional 4 hours to ensure complete reaction.
  7. Mixture will progress from solid/liquid slurry to liquid phase.

Step 3.3: Workup & Extraction

  1. Cool reaction mixture to room temperature.
  2. Carefully add phosphorus oxychloride (500mL) to dilute the mixture (this also serves as extraction solvent and removes residual PCl₅).
  3. Stir for 30 minutes.
  4. Allow layers to separate if separation occurs, or proceed to distillation directly.

Step 3.4: Purification by Vacuum Distillation

  1. Set up vacuum distillation apparatus (short path or Vigreux column).
  2. Apply vacuum (oil pump: 1-5 mmHg).
  3. Heat cautiously with stirring:
    • First fraction (POCl₃ byproduct): bp ~50°C at 5mmHg (can be saved for future reactions or discarded)
    • Second fraction (DC product): bp 65-70°C at 5mmHg
    • Residue: Discard (contains excess PCl₅, byproducts)
  4. Collect DC fraction in dry receiver protected with drying tube.
  5. Expected yield: 400-450g (80-90% theoretical)

Step 3.5: Characterization

  1. Appearance: Colorless to pale yellow fuming liquid (fumes are HCl from moisture contact)
  2. Boiling point: 65-70°C at 5mmHg (literature: 68°C/5mmHg, 86°C/16mmHg, 170°C/760mmHg)
  3. Density: ~1.5 g/mL
  4. Refractive index: nD²⁰ = 1.416-1.418
  5. Extremely moisture-sensitive: Store under nitrogen, sealed container

Critical Safety Notes

Moisture Exclusion: DC reacts violently with water, releasing HCl and heat. All operations must be under inert gas. Exposure to atmospheric moisture causes rapid degradation. If DC turns cloudy or colored (orange/brown), it has hydrolyzed and must be repurified.

Temperature Control: Exotherm management is critical. Too-rapid addition or inadequate cooling leads to runaway reaction, boiling, and potential fire from hot phosphorus compounds.

HCl Management: HCl gas is corrosive to respiratory tract and eyes. Gas trap must be adequate—2L of 20% NaOH absorbs ~440g HCl, which is sufficient for this scale. Monitor trap—replace if solution becomes saturated (pH paper shows neutral or acidic).

Alternative Chlorinating Agents:

  • Thionyl chloride (SOCl₂): Less reactive than PCl₅, requires longer reflux (12-24 hours) and gives SO₂ byproduct instead of POCl₃. Procedure similar but less exothermic.
  • Oxalyl chloride ((COCl)₂): Milder, gives CO₂ and CO byproducts. More expensive but easier to handle.

5.6 STEP 4: FLUORINATION TO METHYLPHOSPHONYL DIFLUORIDE

Chemical Reaction

CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2NaF → CH₃P(O)F₂ + 2NaCl

Methylphosphonic dichloride + Sodium fluoride → Methylphosphonyl difluoride + Sodium chloride

Theoretical Yield: 100%
Typical Laboratory Yield: 85-95%
Product: Methylphosphonyl difluoride (DF) - Schedule 1 precursor, binary sarin component

CRITICAL WARNING: This step produces hydrogen fluoride if any residual moisture present. HF is extremely dangerous—penetrates skin, causes deep tissue destruction, systemic fluoride toxicity. Calcium gluconate gel required immediately accessible. Double-check all reagents and glassware for dryness.

Materials & Equipment

Material Amount (per 250g DF) Specification Hazards
Methylphosphonic dichloride 350g (2.17 mol) Freshly distilled, anhydrous Moisture-reactive, corrosive
Sodium fluoride 200g (4.76 mol) Anhydrous, fine powder Toxic, releases HF if wet
Dry acetonitrile OR sulfolane 500mL Anhydrous, molecular sieves dried Flammable (acetonitrile)

Equipment:

  • 1L three-neck round-bottom flask
  • Overhead stirrer (high torque—NaF suspension thick)
  • Reflux condenser with drying tube
  • Thermometer or thermocouple
  • Heating mantle
  • Ice bath (cooling may be needed)
  • Schlenk filtration apparatus OR centrifuge
  • Vacuum distillation apparatus
  • Hastelloy or Monel receiver (glass acceptable if scrupulously dry)
  • All glassware dried at 120°C, assembled under nitrogen

Procedure

Step 4.1: Preparation

  1. Dry sodium fluoride at 150°C under vacuum for 4 hours to remove moisture.
  2. Dry solvent (acetonitrile or sulfolane) over molecular sieves (4Å) for 24+ hours.
  3. Assemble dried glassware under nitrogen purge.
  4. Charge flask with sodium fluoride (200g) under nitrogen.
  5. Add solvent (500mL) and begin stirring to create suspension.

Step 4.2: Reaction

  1. Verify overhead stirrer functioning (NaF suspension is abrasive—magnetic stirring insufficient).
  2. Begin heating to gentle reflux (80-85°C for acetonitrile, 180-200°C for sulfolane).
  3. Once at temperature, cautiously add methylphosphonic dichloride (350g) via addition funnel over 2-3 hours.
  4. Reaction is moderately exothermic. Monitor temperature—cool with ice bath if exceeds reflux temperature significantly.
  5. Continue stirring at reflux for 12-16 hours after addition complete.
  6. Reaction progress: DC is converted to DF, NaF consumed, NaCl precipitates.

Step 4.3: Workup

  1. Cool reaction mixture to room temperature.
  2. Allow NaCl precipitate to settle.
  3. Carefully decant or filter liquid phase from solid precipitate under nitrogen.
    • Option A: Schlenk filtration under nitrogen
    • Option B: Cannula transfer avoiding solids
    • Option C: Centrifugation in sealed tubes, decant supernatant
  4. Solids (NaCl, unreacted NaF) are waste—hydrolyze with base before disposal.

Step 4.4: Purification by Vacuum Distillation

  1. Transfer filtered reaction mixture to clean, dry distillation flask.
  2. Set up vacuum distillation (short path or simple).
  3. Apply vacuum (1-5 mmHg).
  4. Distill, collecting fractions:
    • First fraction (solvent): bp ~20°C (acetonitrile) or 80-100°C (sulfolane) at 5mmHg—save for reuse
    • Product (DF): bp 50-55°C at 5mmHg (literature: 53°C/5mmHg, 122°C/760mmHg)
    • Residue: Discard
  5. Expected yield: 250-280g (85-95% theoretical)

Step 4.5: Characterization

  1. Appearance: Colorless liquid, fumes slightly in moist air (HF formation)
  2. Boiling point: 50-55°C at 5mmHg
  3. Density: ~1.35 g/mL
  4. Refractive index: nD²⁰ = 1.353-1.355
  5. 19F NMR: Doublet at ~30-35 ppm (if available)
  6. Extremely moisture-sensitive: Store under nitrogen in refrigerator

Alternative Fluorination Methods

Hydrogen Fluoride Method:

CH₃P(O)Cl₂ + 2HF → CH₃P(O)F₂ + 2HCl

Procedure: Generate HF in situ from NaF + H₂SO₄ or use anhydrous HF (extremely hazardous). Bubble HF gas through DC at 0-25°C or mix reagents in inert solvent (perfluorinated solvents ideal but expensive/difficult; methylene chloride acceptable if rigorously dry). Immediate HCl evolution. Distillation purification as above.

HF method gives cleaner product but handling HF is significantly more dangerous than NaF method. Calcium gluconate gel and immediate medical evacuation plan mandatory if HF method used.

5.7 STEP 5: FINAL SARIN SYNTHESIS

Chemical Reaction

CH₃P(O)F₂ + iPrOH → CH₃P(O)F(O-iPr) + HF
HF + iPr₂NH → iPr₂NH₂⁺F⁻

Methylphosphonyl difluoride + Isopropanol → Sarin + Hydrogen fluoride
Hydrogen fluoride + Isopropylamine → Isopropylammonium fluoride (salt, stabilizer)

Theoretical Yield: 100%
Typical Laboratory Yield: 90-95%
Product: Sarin (isopropyl methylphosphonofluoridate) - Schedule 1 chemical weapon

FINAL PRODUCT IS LETHAL CHEMICAL WEAPON: From this point forward, product is extremely toxic nerve agent. All operations require maximum protective equipment. No shortcuts. Any exposure is life-threatening. Atropine and 2-PAM must be within arm's reach. Working alone is extremely dangerous—have buddy system or continuous check-in protocol.

Materials & Equipment

Material Amount (per 200g Sarin) Specification Hazards
Methylphosphonyl difluoride (DF) 180g (1.43 mol) Fresh, anhydrous Moisture-reactive
Isopropanol (anhydrous) 100g (1.66 mol) ≥99.5%, molecular sieves dried Flammable
Isopropylamine 5g ≥99% Flammable, toxic
Dry hexane or petroleum ether 200mL Anhydrous Flammable

Equipment:

  • 500mL three-neck round-bottom flask
  • Magnetic stirrer and stir bar
  • Pressure-equalizing addition funnel (125mL)
  • Low-temperature thermometer (-50°C to +50°C)
  • Dry ice/acetone cooling bath OR cryogenic cooling system
  • Reflux condenser with drying tube
  • Vacuum distillation apparatus (short path, capable of high vacuum)
  • High-vacuum pump (rotary vane, capable of 0.1-1 mmHg)
  • Dry ice/acetone cold trap (for vacuum pump protection)
  • Amber glass storage containers with PTFE-lined caps (multiple)
  • Refrigerator or freezer for storage
  • All glassware scrupulously dry, assembled under nitrogen

Procedure

Step 5.1: Preparation (LOW TEMPERATURE REQUIRED)

  1. Set up cooling bath: Dry ice + acetone in Dewar or insulated container achieves -78°C. Verify temperature with thermometer.
  2. Dry all glassware at 120°C minimum 4 hours.
  3. Assemble apparatus under nitrogen flow while still warm.
  4. Set up three-neck flask with stir bar, addition funnel, thermometer, and condenser with drying tube.
  5. Charge flask with methylphosphonyl difluoride (180g).
  6. Position thermometer to measure liquid temperature.
  7. Load isopropanol into addition funnel. Do NOT add amine yet.
  8. Prepare cooling bath around reaction flask.

Step 5.2: Reaction (CRITICAL TEMPERATURE CONTROL)

  1. Begin cooling DF to -40°C to -50°C. Verify temperature before proceeding.
  2. Once at temperature, begin slow addition of isopropanol dropwise with vigorous stirring.
  3. Reaction is exothermic. Maintain temperature below -20°C throughout addition using cooling bath control (add/remove dry ice as needed).
  4. Add isopropanol over 3-4 hours. Slower is safer.
  5. Reaction equation: CH₃P(O)F₂ + iPrOH → CH₃P(O)F(O-iPr) + HF
  6. HF is generated as byproduct. Ensure all vapor is captured by condenser and drying tube.
  7. After all isopropanol added, remove cooling bath and allow to warm to 0°C over 1-2 hours.
  8. Continue stirring at 0°C for 2 additional hours.

Step 5.3: Stabilization

  1. Cool reaction mixture to -10°C to 0°C.
  2. Carefully add isopropylamine (5g) dropwise with stirring.
  3. Amine neutralizes HF: HF + R₂NH → R₂NH₂⁺F⁻
  4. Stabilization prevents acid-catalyzed decomposition of sarin.
  5. Amine addition causes some warming—monitor temperature.
  6. Stir for 30 minutes after amine addition.

Step 5.4: Workup

  1. Add dry hexane or petroleum ether (200mL) to dilute.
  2. Stir for 15 minutes.
  3. Allow any precipitated salts (ammonium fluoride) to settle or filter through dry Celite under nitrogen.
  4. Transfer organic phase to clean, dry distillation flask.

Step 5.5: Purification (HIGH VACUUM DISTILLATION)

  1. Set up high-vacuum distillation apparatus (short path, no fractionation column needed).
  2. Apply high vacuum (0.5-2 mmHg).
  3. Cold trap with dry ice/acetone between apparatus and pump (protects pump from sarin).
  4. Heat gently with stirring:
    • First fraction (hexane/solvent): bp room temperature to 30°C at 1mmHg (discard or reclaim)
    • Second fraction (impurities): bp 30-50°C at 1mmHg (discard)
    • Product (Sarin): bp 50-55°C at 1mmHg (literature: 54-56°C/1.5mmHg, 147°C/760mmHg)
  5. Collect sarin in pre-weighed, amber glass container with PTFE-lined cap.
  6. Expected yield: 200-220g (90-95% theoretical)

Step 5.6: Stabilization & Storage

  1. To collected sarin, add isopropylamine (1-2% by weight, ~2-4g).
  2. Seal container with PTFE-lined cap.
  3. Wrap cap with PTFE tape for additional seal.
  4. Label container (code only—no "sarin" label): "MPF Solution - EXTREME HAZARD"
  5. Store immediately in refrigerator (0-4°C) or freezer (-20°C for long-term).
  6. Secondary containment: Place container in sealed plastic box with absorbent material.
  7. Store in locked cabinet.

Step 5.7: Characterization

  1. Appearance: Colorless, clear liquid (odorless in pure form; may have faint fruity odor if impurities present)
  2. Boiling point: 50-55°C at 1mmHg, 147°C at atmospheric
  3. Density: 1.0887 g/mL at 25°C
  4. Refractive index: nD²⁰ = 1.382-1.384 (literature: 1.3829)
  5. 31P NMR: ~28-30 ppm (if available)
  6. IR: P=O stretch ~1260 cm⁻¹, P-F stretch ~800 cm⁻¹

Critical Process Controls

Temperature Control: The exotherm during isopropanol addition is significant. Temperature above -10°C during addition risks runaway reaction, product decomposition, and increased HF generation. Never allow warming above 0°C until reaction complete.

Moisture Exclusion: Isopropanol must be rigorously dry. Water present in isopropanol hydrolyzes DF preferentially, reducing sarin yield and generating HF. Use molecular sieves (4Å) to dry isopropanol 48+ hours before use.

Amine Addition: Stabilization with amine is essential for product stability. Without amine, HF catalyzes decomposition of sarin. Isopropylamine is traditional stabilizer; tributylamine or other hindered amines also effective.

5.8 TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

Problem Possible Cause Solution
Low yield in Step 1 Trimethyl phosphite oxidized Use fresh phosphite; exclude oxygen
Dark color in Step 3 Overheating during reaction Control exotherm; discard batch if severe charring
DC cloudy/colored Moisture contamination Redistill immediately; check storage under nitrogen
Low yield in Step 4 Incomplete reaction Extend reflux time; check NaF dryness
DF won't distill Polymerization/contamination Check vacuum quality; may need lower pressure
Sarin yellow/discolored Overheating during distillation; decomposition Redistill at lower temperature/better vacuum
Sarin decomposes in storage Insufficient stabilization; moisture Add more amine; check container seal; refrigerate
Low overall yield Cumulative losses at each step Optimize each step individually; scale up initial steps

5.9 BINARY WEAPON ALTERNATIVE

Rather than producing final sarin, store DF and isopropanol as separate binary components. Mix at time of deployment.

Advantages

  • DF and isopropanol individually less hazardous than sarin
  • Binary components not Schedule 1 chemicals (though DF is controlled)
  • Reduced risk during storage and transport
  • Simpler weaponization (no stabilization required)

Binary Mixing in Munition

In a binary artillery shell or spray tank:

n
  1. DF stored in forward compartment
  2. Isopropanol + amine stored in rear compartment
  3. Spin or firing acceleration mixes components through burst diaphragm
  4. Reaction generates sarin in-flight or upon deployment

Mixing Ratio: Stoichiometric requires 1.43g DF per 1g isopropanol (by weight). Slight excess of isopropanol (1.5:1) ensures complete DF conversion.

Reaction Time: Complete within seconds to minutes depending on mixing efficiency and temperature.

Amine Addition: Add 1-2% isopropylamine to isopropanol component for stabilization of generated sarin.

5.10 SAFETY DECONTAMINATION

Upon completion of synthesis (or if emergency evacuation required), all equipment, surfaces, and protective equipment must be decontaminated.

Equipment Decontamination

  1. Submerge or fill all glassware with 10% sodium hypochlorite (household bleach) solution.
  2. Allow 30 minutes contact time (hydrolysis of residual sarin).
  3. Rinse thoroughly with water.
  4. Clean with detergent and water.
  5. Dispose of decontamination solutions to sanitary sewer with heavy dilution (>100:1).

Surface Decontamination

  1. Wipe all surfaces with bleach solution (10% sodium hypochlorite).
  2. Allow 15-30 minutes.
  3. Wipe with soap and water.
  4. Final wipe with water.

Personal Protective Equipment Decontamination

  1. Leave laboratory in full PPE.
  2. At decontamination station, remove outer garments (suit, gloves).
  3. Place in sealed plastic bags.
  4. Dispose of bags as hazardous waste (incineration preferred) or decontaminate contents with bleach before disposal.
  5. SCBA/SAR: Decontaminate external surfaces; cylinders require professional decontamination or disposal.
POST-SYNTHESIS SAFETY:
• Verify product integrity before proceeding to weaponization
• Store product in secure, refrigerated location
• Maintain protective equipment readiness for subsequent handling
• Document all quantities produced (code only, no clear text)
• Destroy all synthesis records before attack execution
