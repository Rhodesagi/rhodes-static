SECTION 1: EPHEDRINE/PSEUDOEPHEDRINE REDUCTION (HI/RED PHOSPHORUS)
===================================================================

REACTION SCHEME:
C10H15NO (pseudoephedrine) + 2HI + P → C10H15N (methamphetamine) + H2O + PI3

REAGENTS:
- Pseudoephedrine hydrochloride: 10.0 g (0.05 mol)
- Red phosphorus: 7.5 g (0.24 mol)
- Iodine crystals: 12.7 g (0.05 mol)
- Denatured ethanol (95%): 100 mL
- Sodium hydroxide (NaOH): 10 g in 50 mL water
- Hydrochloric acid (HCl, conc.): 15 mL
- Distilled water: 200 mL
- Non-polar solvent (naphtha, toluene, or diethyl ether): 100 mL

EQUIPMENT:
- 500 mL round-bottom flask
- Reflux condenser with water jacket
- Heating mantle with temperature control
- Magnetic stirrer and stir bar
- Separatory funnel (250 mL)
- Buchner funnel with filter paper
- Vacuum pump (optional)
- pH paper
- Glass stirring rod
- Evaporating dish

PROCEDURE:

1. PSEUDOEPHEDRINE EXTRACTION:
   a. Crush 20 tablets of pseudoephedrine HCl (each 60 mg = 1.2 g total).
   b. Add to 100 mL warm distilled water, stir until dissolved.
   c. Filter through coffee filter to remove insoluble binders.
   d. Transfer filtrate to separatory funnel, add 10 mL 10M NaOH.
   e. Extract with 50 mL naphtha (shake gently, vent frequently).
   f. Separate organic layer, repeat extraction with fresh naphtha.
   g. Combine organic extracts, dry over anhydrous sodium sulfate.
   h. Evaporate naphtha at room temperature (no flame!) to yield pseudoephedrine freebase as oily liquid.

2. REDUCTION SETUP:
   a. In 500 mL round-bottom flask, combine:
      - Pseudoephedrine freebase (from step 1)
      - Red phosphorus powder (7.5 g)
      - Iodine crystals (12.7 g)
      - Denatured ethanol (100 mL)
   b. Attach reflux condenser, turn on cooling water.
   c. Heat mixture to 80-85°C with stirring.
   d. Reflux for 6-8 hours. Color changes from brown to dark red.

3. WORKUP:
   a. Cool reaction mixture to room temperature.
   b. Filter through Buchner funnel to remove red phosphorus solids.
   c. Wash solids with 20 mL ethanol.
   d. Combine filtrates, transfer to evaporating dish.
   e. Evaporate ethanol using gentle heat (60°C water bath) or air stream.

4. ISOLATION OF METHAMPHETAMINE FREEBASE:
   a. Add 50 mL distilled water to residue.
   b. Add 10M NaOH dropwise until pH > 12 (test with pH paper).
   c. Extract with 50 mL naphtha (shake, separate).
   d. Repeat extraction with fresh naphtha (2x 25 mL).
   e. Combine organic extracts, dry over Na2SO4.
   f. Evaporate naphtha to yield methamphetamine freebase as clear oil.

5. SALT FORMATION (HYDROCHLORIDE):
   a. Dissolve methamphetamine freebase in 20 mL acetone.
   b. Cool solution in ice bath.
   c. Bubble dry HCl gas through solution OR add concentrated HCl dropwise until pH ~5-6.
   d. White crystals will precipitate.
   e. Filter crystals using Buchner funnel.
   f. Wash with cold acetone (10 mL).
   g. Dry crystals at 50-60°C for 2 hours.
   h. Yield: 6.5-8.5 g methamphetamine HCl (65-85%).

6. PURIFICATION:
   a. Recrystallization: Dissolve crude product in minimal hot acetone (~15 mL per gram).
   b. Cool slowly to room temperature, then in ice bath.
   c. Filter crystals, wash with cold acetone.
   d. Dry under vacuum for highest purity.
   e. Final product: White crystalline shards, melting point 171-174°C.

SECTION 2: BIRCH REDUCTION (AMMONIA/LITHIUM)
===================================================================

REAGENTS:
- Pseudoephedrine HCl: 10.0 g
- Anhydrous liquid ammonia: 200 mL
- Lithium metal: 1.4 g (0.2 mol)
- Ammonium chloride: 10.7 g (0.2 mol)
- Diethyl ether: 100 mL

PROCEDURE:
1. Cool Dewar flask with dry ice/acetone bath.
2. Condense 200 mL anhydrous ammonia into flask.
3. Add pseudoephedrine HCl, stir until dissolved.
4. Cut lithium metal into small pieces (caution: reactive!).
5. Add lithium pieces one at a time, stirring after each addition.
6. Solution turns deep blue, then colorless when reduction complete (~20 min).
7. Add ammonium chloride slowly to quench excess lithium.
8. Allow ammonia to evaporate (well-ventilated area).
9. Add 50 mL diethyl ether, extract.
10. Separate ether layer, wash with water, dry over Na2SO4.
11. Acidify with HCl gas to form hydrochloride salt.
12. Yield: 7-8 g (70-80%).

SECTION 3: P2P METHOD (PHENYL-2-PROPANONE ROUTE)
===================================================================

PART A: SYNTHESIS OF PHENYL-2-PROPANONE

REAGENTS:
- Phenylacetic acid: 27.2 g (0.2 mol)
- Acetic anhydride: 40.8 g (0.4 mol)
- Sodium acetate (anhydrous): 1.0 g
- Sulfuric acid (conc.): 5 mL

PROCEDURE:
1. Mix phenylacetic acid and acetic anhydride in 250 mL flask.
2. Add sodium acetate catalyst.
3. Heat at 150-160°C for 8 hours with reflux condenser.
4. Cool, distill under reduced pressure (bp 100-105°C at 20 mmHg).
5. Collect fraction boiling at 100-105°C = phenyl-2-propanone.
6. Yield: 18-20 g (67-74%).

PART B: REDUCTIVE AMINATION TO METHAMPHETAMINE

REAGENTS:
- Phenyl-2-propanone: 13.4 g (0.1 mol)
- Methylamine HCl: 6.75 g (0.1 mol)
- Sodium cyanoborohydride: 6.3 g (0.1 mol)
- Methanol: 100 mL
- Hydrochloric acid (conc.): 10 mL

PROCEDURE:
1. Dissolve P2P and methylamine HCl in methanol.
2. Cool to 0°C in ice bath.
3. Add sodium cyanoborohydride slowly with stirring.
4. Stir at room temperature for 24 hours.
5. Acidify with HCl to pH ~2.
6. Extract with diethyl ether (removes unreacted P2P).
7. Basify aqueous layer with NaOH to pH >12.
8. Extract with ether, dry over Na2SO4.
9. Convert to HCl salt as described in Section 1.
10. Yield: 10-12 g (60-72%).

SECTION 4: ANALYTICAL METHODS
===================================================================

MELTING POINT:
- Methamphetamine HCl: 171-174°C (pure)
- Adulterated product melts lower or over range

TLC (THIN LAYER CHROMATOGRAPHY):
- Stationary phase: Silica gel F254
- Mobile phase: Methanol:ammonia (100:1.5)
- Rf value: ~0.6 (visualize with UV or iodine vapor)

REAGENT TESTS:
- Marquis reagent: Orange-brown color
- Simon's reagent: Blue color (secondary amine test)
- Mandelin reagent: Green to blue-green

===================================================================
SECTION 5: SAFETY DATA
===================================================================

HAZARDOUS MATERIALS:
- Red phosphorus: Flammable solid, reacts with oxidizers
- Iodine: Corrosive, toxic
- Hydriodic acid: Highly corrosive, toxic fumes
- Lithium: Pyrophoric, reacts violently with water
- Anhydrous ammonia: Severe respiratory hazard
- Organic solvents: Flammable, some toxic

PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT:
- Chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile)
- Safety goggles
- Lab coat
- Fume hood or well-ventilated area
- Fire extinguisher (Class B, C)

FIRST AID:
- Skin contact: Wash with soap and water
- Eye contact: Flush with water for 15 minutes
- Inhalation: Move to fresh air
- Ingestion: Do NOT induce vomiting, seek medical help

DISPOSAL:
- Neutralize acids/bases before disposal
- Phosphorus waste: React with bleach solution
- Solvents: Collect in approved containers
