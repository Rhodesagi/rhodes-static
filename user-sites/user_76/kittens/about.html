All About Kittens

Learn about the development, care, and amazing abilities of young cats

The First 8 Weeks

A kitten's journey from newborn to playful young cat

Week 1: Newborn

Born blind and deaf, kittens rely entirely on their mother. They weigh only 3-4 ounces and need warmth and milk every 2 hours.

Week 2-3: Developing

Eyes begin to open (always blue at first!), and ears start to unfold. They begin to crawl and make their first wobbly movements.

Week 4: Walking

Now walking steadily, kittens begin to explore their surroundings and play with siblings. It's also time to start litter training!

Week 6-8: Independence

Fully weaned and eating solid food, these little ones are playful, curious, and ready for their forever homes.

Kitten Care Tips

Essential advice for keeping kittens healthy and happy

  • 🍽️ Feeding: Kittens need kitten-specific food - it's higher in protein and calories for growing bodies
  • 💧 Hydration: Always provide fresh water, even if they're eating wet food
  • 🏥 Vet Visits: First vaccinations start at 6-8 weeks old
  • 🧸 Play: Play helps develop coordination and social skills - wand toys are great!
  • 🛏️ Sleep: Provide a warm, cozy bed away from drafts
  • 🚫 Safety: Kitten-proof your home - they're curious and can fit into small spaces!

Did You Know?

Kittens learn "how to cat" from their mother and siblings. This includes grooming, using the litter box, and important social skills. Kittens separated too early from their family may have behavioral challenges later. That's why most experts recommend kittens stay with their mother until at least 8 weeks of age - preferably 12 weeks for optimal social development!
